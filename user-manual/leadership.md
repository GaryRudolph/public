# GARY-LEADERSHIP(1) — User Manual

## NAME

**gary-leadership** — leadership philosophy and how work with organizations

## SYNOPSIS

```
gary leadership [--context] [--conviction] [--ownership] [--collaborative]
```

## OPTIONS

**--context**
: Always set a clear end vision, the steps to achieve it and the context surrounding it. Every
  person in an organization makes critical decisions in their day-to-day work that impacts
  success. The more they understand the greater chance those decisions will be aligned with our
  vision and strategy.

**--conviction**
: Brings conviction to the organization by following "Strong Opinions, Lightly Held" so that
  we can execute with confidence, but still remain open to better ideas. Always commit to be
  rational and logical. If something doesn't make sense, challenge it and seek to understand —
  sometimes it may be "from a certain point of view" or a systems view.

**--ownership**
: Encourages a safe environment to *"Ask for Forgiveness"* and provide each other grace
  when we blow it and calibrate after — this prevents bottlenecks and creates high agency.
  Demonstrate "End to End" ownership, even beyond your area of scope. We all succeed and
  fail as a team.

**--collaborative**
: Build with teams, not for them. Seek diverse perspectives early — the best solutions come
  from combining viewpoints across disciplines. Create an environment where people feel safe
  to challenge ideas, including mine, and where the best idea wins regardless of who brought it.
  Organizations scale through empowerment, not command and control — push decisions to the
  people closest to the work and give them the context to make good ones.
