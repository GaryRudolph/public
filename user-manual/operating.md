# GARY-OPERATING(1) — User Manual

## NAME

**gary-operating** — how I think about building things

## SYNOPSIS

```
gary operating [--intentional] [--reuse] [--sile] [--mvb]
```

## OPTIONS

**--intentional**
: Build everything with intentionality and a strategy behind it. To do this, widen and expand
  the breadth of the problem at hand. Then extend the time horizon and think beyond the
  immediate needs. With this larger context create a vision and long-term plan, and then, most
  importantly, narrow it back down to what you need today. Play the "long game". The skill is
  to not over-analyze by applying judgement to skip items that are low risk or easy to change
  as we execute.

**--reuse**
: Build for today with tomorrow in mind. If overhead to build reuse is less than 10%, just do
  it. If effort is greater than 10%, be intentional — requires that vision and long-term plan.

**--sile**
: Solve It Like an Engineer. Apply DRY and data/system-driven thinking to everything — people,
  process, software design, communication channels. See [eng-levels.xlsx](eng-levels.xlsx) for
  an example — engineering levels treated as a spec sheet with measurable criteria rather than
  vibes.

**--mvb**
: Minimal Viable Bureaucracy. Just enough process to keep 80% of the chaos at bay while still
  allowing for 20% chaos. If there's no chaos at all, there's likely too much bureaucracy.
  "Minimal" is from the system perspective and may not feel minimal for an individual or team.
